    Round 1, Match 1: Revendread Slayer vs Eldlich the Golden Lord

    Vengeance.

     

    That is the only desire the Slayer had. To completely eradicate everything which once opposed him. To completely annihilate any evil. To slay all which stood in his way. All for punishment. It was a wish so all-consuming, that it was frankly overwhelming!!

     

    He stepped into the arena with a quick hop, his talons already looking for their next victim. The Slayer, now more than any other time, could not show mercy. Becoming judge, jury and executioner was the only way he would be able to survive. He knew that well. Though there was a part of him that wondered whether or not this was the right thing to do. 

     

    After all, his beloved wished for nothing but good from everyone. Most of all himself. 

     

    Then he remembered what those things did to her, and that he might be able to get her back: if only he wins this tournament. If only he becomes the Creator of his own new world. Where nobody needs to suffer, and nobody needs to be punished. Where his job might be over. There was yet another smaller part of him which wondered if that was what he really wanted… or if he simply wanted to keep killing.

     

    Whoa, the referee, a young Tour Guide From The Underworld thought, this guy's serious business. Is he some sorta demon like yours truly…?

     

    The young woman looked around. While the Slayer was here, his opponent was running a little bit late. This would prove itself a problem: there was an audience from people across countless different world waiting to watch this match in the stands - and not to mention, this was the first fight of the night. The crowd was already anxious, some were already rowdy, and some already knew what was happening from the second they saw the bracket.

     

    Four competitors - the mighty Ultra Athlete, the jester of Despia, the Dragonmaid, and the demon hunter known as "Engraver" had the same thought all at once. While they felt differently about it, they all knew what was happening pretty much immediately.

     

    That guy's showing off…

     

    The Slayer looked towards the referee - the Tour Guide. He didn't look happy, but frankly, when did he ever? 

     

    "Where is he?" It seems of all people, the executioner could comprehend this the least out of everyone. However, he did not demand a disqualification, though it was well within his right to. He wished for the taste of blood - and these few minutes of waiting made his own boil in a deep fury. If he couldn't get his meal now, he would feast on whatever was closest: which just so happened to be the referee.

     

    If the Slayer comprehended this least of all, then the Tour Guide understood this just 2% more than he did, which was still not enough. Doesn't he get that MY life and HIS life is at stake? Yet, she spoke: "Um… h-he's coming out soon! If he's not out by the next thirty seconds, I'll make sure he's disqualified, and you can get the win--"

     

    "No," the Slayer commanded. "I wish for combat."

     

    What an annoying guy, Leviathan, another competitor in the crowd, thought. But then, before the Slayer could speak another word more…

     

    He appeared. To call him a brilliant man would be an understatement!! To call him "shiny" would simply be an insult!! He is a man who is literally made of gold!! He is a lord who commands greed itself!! When he walks into the arena, the Golden Lord Eldlich looks rather bored. In fact, he can't contain his own little yawn that came out of his mouth!!

     

    Yep, the demon hunter known as "Engraver" thought to himself, this guy's definitely showin' off. That's gonna be a fun one to watch!

     

    "Apologies for the delay, darlings," The Golden Lord waved a hand dismissively - every man, woman, and child could tell that he wasn't really sorry!! "I'd been busy with something much more important than this little squabble. My beauty sleep, of course. I require 12 hours of it every day, even when I'm in the middle of something like this. You understand, yes?" He grins; even his teeth are made of some shiny material. "Shall we begin?"

     

    "Greed," the Slayer hissed under his breath, "luxury, lust… you'll pay for all your sins, even if I have to drag your head through the dirt."

     

    "Ohoho. So you've wished to compliment me just before a fight?" Eldlich smirked. It was hard to comprehend what he was behind that mask - behind those teeth. "Do know there are other ways to pay tribute than buttering me up, darling! Love, money…"

     

    "Um-- let's," the Tour Guide interrupted both of them. The tension was too high - at least from one of these two. "Let's begin! The first match of the first round: Revendread Slayer versus Eldlich the Golden Lord!!"

     

    The Slayer was the first to move - he hopped through the air like it was butter, and one of his talons on his back cut quickly through the distance, reaching the Golden Lord with little to no issue. 

     

    "He's fast," Raye, one of the competitors in the crowd, commented to another person who's watching the fight. Though, maybe deep down, she was simply being polite. Especially because:

     

    "It doesn't matter either way," the golden swordswoman next to Raye replied, tersely. It seems that to her, this fight was already over.

     

    An experienced fighter would know this a million times: there is a difference between reaching a moving target quickly and hitting a target which simply refuses to move. When the talons attack the Golden Lord viciously, Eldlich simply does not take a single step. He stands right there and takes it. Though the curious thing is this: the talons do not pierce through his armor. In fact, with a CLINK noise, they kind of bounce off and get at an awkward angle… 

     

    … of which Eldlich simply grabs one of them with one hand. "It's too simple. Kind of like you, actually, Slayer boy!"

     

    The Slayer's talons were his greatest weapon. They were the weapons of which he used to kill. Covered in a deep venom of rage, it was impossible that someone could just grab them like this without getting cut and killed right then and there - yet Eldlich remained there unbothered…

     

    Too simple? What did he mean by that? What is he? What is he going to do next? What should I do? Should I retract my blades? If I do that, I'll be defenseless for a few seconds. The distance is too close. I'm not afraid of the pain. I don't think he can hurt me. I should brace myself--

     

    "So I guess I'll be a simpleton in return," Eldlich said, nearly sweetly but with a tiny hint of boredom, and then slammed the Slayer onto the ground with his entire force. It made a reverberating noise, and as the Slayer hit concrete, it was enough to also hear a crack from the ground.

     

    That's when it became clear to everyone watching. The difference in size was also great. The Slayer looked tall and intimidating, but the Golden Lord made him look outright short. When he slammed him into the ground, it was a much bigger man bullying a much smaller one, and that slam - he could hear something break, a sweet melody to Eldlich.

     

    It hurts, it hurts, it hurts, it hurts, it hurts - what do I do, what do I do, what do I do - think of the next move - kill him, kill him, kill him! Attack him when he comes close! There is a greater distance! Use the shadows!

     

    Eldlich, however, seemed wholly unconcerned with closing the distance in a quick manner. He wished to simply take his time and parade around the arena as if he owned it. In a way, every spectator there … at least for a brief moment … believed it!! They believed him!! For this one brief moment in time… the Golden Lord also possessed this entire arena, so he can walk in any way that he wants in it!! So he takes his sweet time approaching him! There is no fear whatsoever coming from him!!

     

    The Slayer was a being of the shadows. He existed in the darkness! So he wished to surprise Eldlich with an underhanded attack - a spear of the night! A spear made of his shadow! Instead of attacking with his talons, he retracted them as the flamboyant man approached, and simply waited. It took a few seconds. Agonizing seconds. But he heard the noise. It was easy to hear the noise from the Golden Lord - he was literally made out of gold, and every time he took a step, it was practically an event. So even though … the Slayer was tucked into this small little crater, he could predict his movements fine!

     

    And when he finally arrived… the Slayer looked upwards, and threw his spear towards the Eldlich's head. It is no contest - it struck him cleanly, piercing through the Golden Lord's skull. The helmet didn't tear apart; it was too heavy for that, but it was enough for it to go through cleanly. Though it was underhanded, it didn't matter. He did what he had to do to win!! Nobody could complain about that!!

     

    Though his spine was certainly twisted now, he managed to bring himself vertically once more. "I was expecting more," the Slayer said, trying to maintain his composure. "This fight is--"

     

    "You hurt my beautiful face," the Golden Lord spat out, through his mask. Though the spear of darkness was still cleanly still stuck there, "Unforgivable. Simply… unforgivable." He still managed to speak fine! It was like he wasn't even that hurt!!

     

    That is when, that is when, that is when!! That is when the Slayer realized!! The Golden Lord was one of them, too! He was… undead!

     

    This is good, the Slayer thought. All of this thinking took around 2 seconds to produce -- the thoughts of an experienced fighter! I can hurt him if he's undead. But he didn't seem hurt by my spear. I'll strike with the shadows again. That'd be--

     

    The person who understood what happened next the quickest was another contestant.

     

    Kentregina was thinking that this is the most boring match she'd ever watched in her entire life. Because it was simply too one-sided. The reason why…

     

    Is that deep down…

     

    Doesn't matter if this idiot's got shadow powers or whatever…

     

    This Revendread Slayer guy's just another human wearing a silly costume.

     

    It connected cleanly and directly, the fist of the Golden Lord with the Slayer's face. It happened before he could react. It struck heavily against his mask, and sent him flying towards the wall, and nearly through it. A few more bones broke, enough to make another satisfying sound. Yet, Eldlich advanced. Before, his slow movement was simply because he couldn't be bothered. Now, he wished to savor every moment of bringing him pain!!

     

    So Eldlich approached slowly once more! But… deep down, the Slayer was panicking. Getting slammed and punched in the face like that showed that the difference in power was simply too great. The Golden Lord was actually moving deliberately slow; he could move much faster than what he usually was. So he had to plan quickly! If a shotgun wouldn't work, go for a machine gun! If a machine gun doesn't work, go for a bazooka! He just had to throw everything he had at this monster! 

     

    Slayer tried to bring himself back upwards, and tried to make his blood boil once more - he threw spear after spear of the deepest darkest shadow towards the Golden Lord, all of which pierced through his armor and were stuck there like big pin-cushions as he walked. Yet, the Golden Lord continued walking!! He could not be killed!!

     

    "... It's just too simple," Eldlich hissed. "Someone who wishes for just one thing… can never hope to beat someone who wants everything."

     

    Then the Slayer realized: he was already too close. The Golden Lord loomed over the human. Despite this, the Slayer was the one who struck first, one of his blades piercing through the Golden Lord's chest … 

     

    "Slayer boy," Eldlich began winding up his punch like it was some sort of silly cartoon, "I'll give you a C for effort. See~ me~ after~ class!"

     

    And then punched him directly in the face. It was enough to cave his head in, and make the Slayer fall down to the ground, collapsed like he was almost melting. He was unconscious - but the Golden Lord didn't kill him.

     

    Yet, even though he's covered in spears… he still couldn't be killed. What could kill him?

     

    The King of the Skull Servants seemed to approach at an answer. So did Mikailis…

     

    And a few others as well. A few didn't…

     

    But what mattered was this: the Golden Lord bowed at the crowd, and put on a good show, enough to make the group of people watching erupt into screams.

     

    Seems he's popular, Engraver thought, This guy's gonna be a problem…

     

    Maybe working for him wouldn't be so bad? Aluber smiled.

     

    "Th-The winner is! The winner of the first match of the first round is Eldlich, the Golden Lord!" Tour Guide announced to everybody, her mic peaking for the first time.
